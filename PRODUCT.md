# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Community members, local advocates, and "place people" in post-industrial / post-war towns
(Buffalo, Youngstown, and outward — eventually US/AU/JP/IT), plus researchers, urbanists, and
local councils. They arrive curious or civic-minded, often on mobile, wanting to (a) see what
dormant land, buildings, and civic assets exist near them, and (b) add the ones they know about.
Low friction, no account needed to contribute. A secondary audience (the owner/admin) curates
submissions and watches the commons grow.

## Product Purpose

CloudtoTerra — *from cloud to land* — is an open, public-good crowd map of dormant, distressed,
and underused urban assets waiting to be reactivated. The online crowd surfaces real places; a
review gate keeps the map trustworthy; the resulting dataset is a free commons (CC-BY) that also
feeds curated signal into the commercial Nubis platform. Success = people trust it enough to
contribute, browse, and cite it; the map visibly fills in and tells a "why here" story.

CloudtoTerra is the public-good sibling of Nubis (commercial distressed-asset intelligence). It
must feel like a civic commons, not a product being sold.

## Brand Personality

Cartographic and exploratory. Three words: **surveyor, civic, hopeful.** You are charting
overlooked places — the tone is that of a field atlas or a survey notebook: precise, grounded,
quietly optimistic. Confident but never slick; warm but never twee. Voice is plain and concrete
(names places, counts things), never salesy or hype-driven. It should feel like a tool made by
people who love these towns, not a startup flipping them.

## Anti-references

- **Real-estate SaaS** (Zillow / CoStar / Fundrise): cap-rate cards, stock hero photography,
  blue-gray corporate sameness. CloudtoTerra is a commons, not a listings funnel.
- **Crypto / web3**: neon-on-black, glassmorphism, gradient hype. The opposite of speculative.
- **Startup template**: cream background + purple gradient + identical feature-card grid + pill
  buttons everywhere. The default AI-SaaS look. Avoid on sight.
- **Corporate fintech**: navy + gold, institutional-cold. Wrong for a grassroots commons.

## Design Principles

1. **The map is the argument.** Cartography is the identity, not decoration. Legends, graticules,
   coordinates, survey markers, contour linework carry meaning and brand at once.
2. **Paper, not chrome.** Warm bone surfaces and sepia ink over glass/shadow/gradient. Hairlines
   and layered paper instead of drop-shadowed cards.
3. **Name the real.** Show concrete places, counts, coordinates, conditions. Specificity earns
   trust; vagueness reads as a sales funnel.
4. **From cloud to land.** The thesis is literal and visual: cool "cloud" above resolving into
   warm "terra" below. Use it sparingly and intentionally (hero, transitions), never as wallpaper.
5. **Commons over conversion.** Every surface should feel public, open, and additive — invite
   contribution, not a signup wall.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Ink-on-paper contrast must pass AA for body and large text (verify the sepia ink and
muted-label tones). Map information never by colour alone — pair pin colour with glyph/shape and
text (already true). Full keyboard operability incl. map markers and the submission modal; visible
focus rings (ink, not removed). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` (disable plot/draw + fades).
Mobile-first: contributors are often on phones in the field.
